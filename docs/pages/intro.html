Background

The dataset that we chose is a UK Consumer Dataset from the years 1997 - 2022. This is a vast dataset so we had narrow down what areas of the data we sought to analyse. We decided to only anaylse the data between the years 1997 and 2017 as we felt as if 20 years was a sufficient sample period of time. Another reason fro omitting the years from 2018 - 2022 was the COVID-19 pandemic that shock the global economy, subsequently affecting the consumer spending.

Hypothesis

We aim to investigate how time of year affects consumer spending across different categories. We aim to investigate 3 categories in total. As a by-product of our investigation we should be able to observe a steady rise in spending since 1997. This rise can be attributed to many factors with the main one being inflation.

Research

We utilized kaggle to peruse a vast range of datasets until we found ours. We looked at many datasets including: Chicago Crime Stats, Student SAT scores and 2016 election exit polls. We landed on our Consumer dataset in the end as we believed that it contained a sufficient amount of data to anaylse multiple areas of the topic. An attribute which the others lacked.
